day guide

A researched, heat-aware plan with the useful part first: how to move between the airport, hotels, cities, and day trips. Exact departure times should be rechecked in Japan.

Arrival · Aug 2

Narita → Asakusa

  1. After immigration, follow Train signs to the Keisei station in your terminal.
  2. Use Suica or buy a ticket for Asakusa. Take the Access Express bound for Oshiage, Asakusa, or Haneda Airport.
  3. If your train goes toward Ueno instead, change at Aoto for an Asakusa-line through train.
  4. Exit at Toei Asakusa Station, then walk about 10 minutes to Ryokan Asakusa Shigetsu.
Keisei fares ↗Live route ↗
Transfer · Aug 4

Tokyo → Osaka

  1. Walk to Asakusa Station; take the Ginza Line to Kanda, then JR one stop to Tokyo Station. With luggage, a taxi to Tokyo Station is simpler.
  2. Take a reserved Nozomi Tokaido Shinkansen from Tokyo to Shin-Osaka, about 2 hours 20 minutes.
  3. At Shin-Osaka, take the Osaka Metro Midosuji Line to Shinsaibashi.
  4. Walk about 7 minutes to Hotel The Flag.
Book SmartEX ↗Live route ↗
Transfer · Aug 9

Osaka → Shimokitazawa

  1. Take the Midosuji Line from Shinsaibashi to Shin-Osaka.
  2. Take a reserved Nozomi to Shinagawa.
  3. Take the JR Yamanote Line to Shibuya, then the Keio Inokashira Line to Shimokitazawa.
  4. Walk to Mustard Hotel Shimokitazawa. Allow extra time for the Shibuya transfer with luggage.
SmartEX ↗Live route ↗
Departure · Aug 18

Shinjuku → Narita

  1. Leave luggage with the hotel after checkout and have an early lunch nearby.
  2. Walk to JR Shinjuku Station and follow signs for the Narita Express.
  3. Take a reserved N’EX directly to your Narita terminal. Target a train around 2:00 PM.
  4. Aim to reach the airport by 3:30 PM for the 6:30 PM international flight.

Aug 2TokyoArrival + Asakusa

Plan

  • Arrive NRT at 3:00 PM; allow 60–90 minutes for immigration, baggage, and customs.
  • Check in, shower, and keep the evening intentionally light.
  • Walk through Sensō-ji after sunset and eat near the ryokan.

Getting there

  • Keisei Access Express to Asakusa; follow the arrival route above.
  • Use the Toei Asakusa station—not Tsukuba Express Asakusa—for the easiest airport connection.
Aug 3Tokyo eastAsakusa + Ueno

Plan

  • 7:30 Sensō-ji and quiet backstreets before the crowds.
  • Breakfast, then Kappabashi kitchenware street.
  • Indoor lunch and rest during peak heat.
  • Late afternoon Ueno Park and Ameyoko; skip museums if closed Monday.

Getting around

  • Walk around Asakusa and Kappabashi.
  • Ginza Line: Tawaramachi or Asakusa → Ueno.
  • Ginza Line back to Asakusa after dinner.
Aug 4OsakaShinkansen + Dotonbori

Plan

  • Check out and take a late-morning Shinkansen.
  • Check in at The Flag and rest.
  • Explore Shinsaibashi and Dotonbori after sunset.

Getting there

  • Follow the Tokyo → Osaka route above.
  • Reserve the Shinkansen in SmartEX; a mid-morning train avoids rush hour.
Aug 5OsakaMarkets + old Osaka

Plan

  • Kuromon Market in the morning.
  • Namba Yasaka Shrine, then indoor lunch.
  • Late afternoon Shinsekai and Tsutenkaku area.
  • Chibo okonomiyaki or a small izakaya for dinner.

Getting around

  • Walk or take one Midosuji Line stop: Shinsaibashi → Namba.
  • From Namba, use the Sakaisuji Line area or walk south toward Shinsekai.
Aug 6KyotoFushimi Inari + Gion

Plan

  • Leave early and reach Fushimi Inari before 8:00 AM.
  • Walk only as far up the torii route as the heat allows.
  • Long indoor lunch/rest near Kyoto Station or Kawaramachi.
  • Higashiyama lanes and Gion in the cooler late afternoon.

Getting there

  • Midosuji Line: Shinsaibashi → Umeda; walk to JR Osaka Station.
  • JR Special Rapid to Kyoto, then JR Nara Line to Inari.
  • For Gion, use Keihan from Fushimi-Inari to Gion-Shijo; return via Kyoto or Osaka.
  • Avoid buses when possible; Kyoto’s official guide recommends rail for Fushimi.
Aug 7OsakaBirthday, slow + special

Plan

  • Sleep in and have a relaxed breakfast.
  • Choose one indoor anchor: Osaka Aquarium Kaiyukan or shopping/cafés in Umeda.
  • Return to the hotel to reset before dinner.
  • Birthday dinner—reserve in advance.

Getting around

  • For Kaiyukan: Midosuji to Hommachi, transfer to Chuo Line for Osakako.
  • For Umeda: direct Midosuji Line from Shinsaibashi.
Aug 8NaraNara Park half-day

Plan

  • Leave early for Nara Park before the strongest heat.
  • Tōdai-ji and the Great Buddha.
  • Lunch in Naramachi, then return to Osaka mid-afternoon.
  • Final Osaka evening near the hotel.

Getting there

  • Midosuji Line: Shinsaibashi → Namba; walk to Osaka-Namba.
  • Kintetsu Nara Line Rapid Express to Kintetsu-Nara, then walk to Nara Park.
  • Suica works on JR and Kintetsu routes; check the live route that morning.
Aug 9TokyoReturn + Shimokitazawa

Plan

  • Travel after breakfast and check in at Mustard Hotel.
  • Stay local: thrift shops, record stores, cafés, and dinner nearby.

Getting there

  • Follow the Osaka → Shimokitazawa route above.
  • Reserve seats before the weekend; keep luggage compact for Shibuya.
Aug 10Tokyo westShimokitazawa slow day

Plan

  • Late breakfast and neighborhood walk.
  • Thrift shops and vinyl stores as they open.
  • Indoor afternoon break, then live music or a small bar at night.

Getting around

  • Everything is walkable from Mustard Hotel.
  • No cross-city travel today—use it as a recovery day.
Aug 11National holidayNakameguro + Daikanyama

Plan

  • Walk the Meguro River in the morning.
  • Daikanyama T-Site and nearby design shops.
  • Lunch/coffee, then continue on foot toward Ebisu if energy allows.

Getting there

  • Keio Inokashira Line: Shimokitazawa → Shibuya.
  • Tokyu Toyoko Line: Shibuya → Nakameguro.
  • Walk Nakameguro → Daikanyama → Ebisu; return via Shibuya.
  • Mountain Day: expect holiday crowds and verify shop hours.
Aug 12TokyoMeiji + Harajuku

Plan

  • Meiji Jingu early while the forest is cooler.
  • Harajuku and Takeshita Street late morning.
  • Indoor lunch, then Omotesando architecture and Café Reissue.

Getting there

  • Keio Inokashira Line to Shibuya.
  • JR Yamanote Line one stop to Harajuku.
  • Walk Meiji Jingu → Harajuku → Omotesando; return from Omote-sando or Shibuya.
Aug 13ShinjukuHotel move + city views

Plan

  • Move to Blossom Shinjuku and leave bags if the room is not ready.
  • Lunch, department stores, and the free Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building view.
  • Omoide Yokocho or a calmer nearby dinner.

Getting there

  • Odakyu Line: Shimokitazawa → Shinjuku, about 10 minutes on an express.
  • Use the South Exit area and walk to the hotel.
  • Obon begins: staying within Tokyo avoids the busiest long-distance travel.
Aug 14Tokyo eastUeno + Akihabara

Plan

  • Tokyo National Museum or another Ueno museum in the morning.
  • Ueno Park and Ameyoko lunch.
  • Akihabara electronics, arcades, and camera shops late afternoon.

Getting there

  • JR Yamanote Line: Shinjuku → Ueno.
  • JR Yamanote/Keihin-Tōhoku Line: Ueno → Akihabara.
  • Return directly to Shinjuku on JR Chuo-Sobu or Yamanote.
Aug 15West TokyoMusashino + Kichijoji

Plan

  • Edo-Tokyo Open Air Architectural Museum in the morning.
  • Indoor lunch and rest.
  • Kichijoji and Inokashira Park toward sunset.

Getting there

  • JR Chuo Line: Shinjuku → Musashi-Koganei, then local bus to the museum.
  • JR Chuo Line onward to Kichijoji.
  • Return Kichijoji → Shinjuku directly on the Chuo Line.
  • Check museum hours before going; August 15 is within Obon.
Aug 16Tokyo centralTsukiji + Ginza

Plan

  • Tsukiji Outer Market early for breakfast.
  • Walk to Ginza for air-conditioned shops and galleries.
  • Tokyo Station/Marunouchi near sunset, then dinner.

Getting there

  • Toei Oedo Line: Shinjuku-nishiguchi → Tsukijishijo.
  • Walk Tsukiji → Ginza; then Tokyo Metro Marunouchi Line or walk to Tokyo Station.
  • Marunouchi Line direct back to Shinjuku.
Aug 17Tokyo westKoenji + final shopping

Plan

  • Arrive around noon, when the independent shops begin opening.
  • Start south of the station: PAL and Look shopping streets.
  • Browse vintage clothing, record shops, and the smaller side streets.
  • Stay for an early izakaya dinner, then return to pack.

Getting there

  • JR Chuo-Sobu Local: Shinjuku → Koenji, roughly 10 minutes.
  • Use the South Exit for PAL and Look; explore the north side later if energy allows.
  • Take the same local line back to Shinjuku.
  • Check Monday opening hours and respect no-photo signs inside small shops and venues.
Aug 18DepartureShinjuku to Miami

Plan

  • Early lunch and collect bags around 1:30 PM.
  • N’EX around 2:00 PM; airport target 3:30 PM.
  • Fly NRT → DFW → MIA at 6:30 PM.

Getting there

  • Follow the Shinjuku → Narita route above.
  • Verify your terminal, N’EX departure, and airline check-in cutoff the day before.

vinyl
digging

The strongest record-store clusters already fit the itinerary. Start after noon—many independent shops open late—and check each store on the day before crossing the city for it.

Osaka · Amemura + Minamihorie

Deep first dig

Aug 5 or 7
  • Time Bomb ↗

    Punk, psych, new wave, soul, ska, rockabilly and Japanese rock. Excellent curated stop.

  • Groovenut ↗

    Soul, funk, jazz, disco, hip-hop and R&B—one of the priority shops for groove-heavy digging.

  • King Kong ↗

    Large, long-running all-genre shop with affordable bins, Japanese music and rarer records.

  • Newtone ↗

    Electronic, experimental, ambient, house and left-field music. More selective, less obvious.

Tokyo · Shimokitazawa

Near the hotel

Aug 10
  • Disk Union ↗

    The broad first stop: used rock, Japanese music, hip-hop, R&B, house and techno with reliable grading.

  • Pianola ↗

    Experimental, ambient, contemporary, global and independent releases in Bonus Track.

  • Flash Disc Ranch ↗

    Old-school, densely packed used-vinyl experience. Rock, jazz, soul and the pleasure of patient crate digging.

Tokyo · Koenji

Underground route

Aug 17
  • EAD ↗

    Rare groove, jazz-funk, disco, house, Japanese synth, fusion, ambient and soundtracks.

  • Uptown ↗

    Experimental, synth, avant-garde, post-punk, noise and Chinese underground music.

  • Record Boy ↗

    A Koenji institution for hardcore punk, metal and obscure Japanese underground releases.

  • BE-IN ↗

    Collector-grade used vinyl, especially Beatles, Stones and scarce UK pressings.

Tokyo · Shibuya + Shinjuku

Big inventory

Aug 12 or 14
  • HMV Shibuya ↗

    Around 80,000 records: Japanese pop and rock downstairs; jazz, soul, hip-hop, R&B and dance upstairs.

  • Face Records ↗

    Clean, accessible selection of soul, jazz, city pop, Japanese pressings and collectible titles.

  • Disk Union Shinjuku ↗

    Multiple specialist shops and floors. Choose by genre instead of attempting the entire complex.

Digging strategy

Prioritize the “new arrivals” bins, check the vinyl grading and pressing details, and ask before using a listening station. Save heavy purchases for the final Tokyo days—or carry a rigid record mailer so the sleeves survive the flight.
